Cork Dork → The Stonehaus → WeVINO — three legendary spots within 10 minutes of each other. Do all three in a week.

Westlake Village · Wine Bar & Restaurant

Dog-Friendly Adventures
March is peak hiking season in Southern California — wildflowers, green hills, and comfortable temperatures. These are all dog-friendly or dog-welcoming spots.
🌸
March is Prime Time for Trails
After winter rains, the Conejo Valley hills are brilliantly green with wildflowers in bloom. Paradise Falls will be running strong. This is the absolute best time of year to hike here.
💧
⭐ 4.7
Paradise Falls – Wildwood Park
928 W Avenida De Los Arboles, Thousand Oaks
The crown jewel of local hikes. A beautiful 3–4.6 mile trek through open meadows ending at a 30-foot waterfall. Well-marked, dogs welcome. After March rains, the falls will be spectacular. A must-do first week.
Hike
Waterfall
Dog-Friendly
🦌
⭐ 4.8
Rancho Sierra Vista / Satwiwa
4121 Potrero Rd, Newbury Park
Vast open meadows with mountain views leading to a serene 100-foot waterfall. Frogs, newts, and incredible wildlife in March. Easy to moderate — choose your intensity. Reviewers call it "perfect for a first date." 😊
Hike
Wildlife
🌿
⭐ 4.7
Los Robles Trail & Open Space
351 S Moorpark Rd, Thousand Oaks
9+ miles of trails from Westlake to Newbury Park, with highlights at Angel Vista and Space Mountain. Dogs explicitly mentioned as welcome. Shaded sections — great for Benji on warmer afternoons.
Hike
Dog-Friendly
🦅
⭐ 4.6
Conejo Canyons Trail Head
2010 Conejo Center Dr, Thousand Oaks
Beautiful trails with spring wildflowers in full bloom in March. Reviewers say "So many wildflowers — beautiful time of year." Multiple split-off trails. Coyote and deer sightings common. Green and stunning post-rain.
Hike
Wildflowers

⭐ 4.7
Autumn Ridge Trail
Thousand Oaks / Simi Hills
3.5 to 7+ mile options with panoramic views. Dogs welcome and frequently seen on trail. In March after rains, reviewers say a pond springs up with mallard ducks and wildflowers everywhere. Truly beautiful.
Hike
Dog-Friendly

Conejo Creek North Park
1379 E Janss Rd, Thousand Oaks
A beautiful community park with duck pond, turtles, BBQ areas, and walking paths. Pet-friendly and great for a relaxed afternoon with both dogs after a morning hike. Also works for a family picnic.

Point Dume State Beach
Malibu · ~30 min drive
Dog-friendly beach sections in Malibu. March is whale migration season — you may spot gray whales from the bluffs! Combine with a hike on the Point Dume Nature Preserve trails with the dogs. Spectacular views.
